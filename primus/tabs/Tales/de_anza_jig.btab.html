Correction by David Johnson. 

I think there are a few mistakes in the tab.
The verse has 2 more notes in it, it should be:

|-----------\9999-7-------|-\9999-7-----------------|
|--------10---------10----|---------10--------------|
|---7-10---------------10-|------------10--5-55-7-5-|
|-8-----------------------|-------------------------|

|-----------\9999-7-------|-9-7-------------------------|
|--------10---------10----|-----10--10-7----------------|
|---7-10---------------10-|--------------10--10-7-------|
|-8-----------------------|-----------------------10--8-|

The reason there is one more note in the beginning, is that he plays
the same beats as the banjo: tum - da, tum - da, etc...  If you listen
carefully you will hear him play 4 notes before he plays the 16th
notes.  If you only play 3 notes you get a time-gap.

And when he goes back up, playing |-5-55-7-5-|, he also plays 2 16th
notes: the '-55-'.  The first 16th note could be a muted note, but
this won't make a lot of difference when you're playing fast.

I also added a slide before the 16th notes (see \ ).  I think I saw
Les Claypool play a slide at a concert I saw.  He does play an upright
on this song which makes it sound better when you slide to higher
notes.

But then again I could be wrong about all of this, that's why I am
asking you to look at my idea's and give me some feadback. If the tab
is right don't kill me, instead explain me why.
