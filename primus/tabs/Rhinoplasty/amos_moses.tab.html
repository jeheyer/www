Three months have passed and I am still adamant that what I wrote is
indeed what Ler is playing.  The bridge that Adam Mckinely tabbed is
exact sounding (and is where Ler most likely played it based on the
electric guitar background that he has). However I would call it a
pre-chorus part.

The second rhythm part during the verses is as follows (traditional
barring style):

                E7 D7 A7 E7 D7 A7 E7 D7 C (hold 2 beats and then the pre-chorus)

I'm stealing from Adam Mckinely but here is the pre-chorus:

                        |-------------------------------------------------------------
                        |-------------------------8------------------8----------------
                        |-----------5--------------------------5--------7-h-5p--------
                        |--5-/-7--------5-/-7--------5-/-7------------------------7---
                        |-------------------------------------------------------------
                        |-------------------------------------------------------------

Then just before going back to the opening lick the above leads into a
bass part that sounds good on guitar and makes the song sound complete
if you are not playing with any accompaniament:

                        l-------------------------------------------------------------
                        l-------------------------------------------------------------
                        l-------------------------------------------------------------
                        l--------0----------------------------------------------------
                        l-----------0-------------------------------------------------
                        l-----7-------------------------------------------------------

In order to get the rhythm right in the second measure, it is
important to follow the stroking pattern as shown above.  The whole
song is about 100 beats a minute.  To play the rhythm part during the
verses (playing the EADB strings):


A7 --x------ B7 --x------ D7 --x--------
   --x------    --x------    --x--------
   ----6----    ----8----    ----11-----
