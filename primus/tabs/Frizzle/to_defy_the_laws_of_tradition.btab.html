Tabbed by Jeffrey Swing.

Notes on notation: Since flamenco style strumming is a big part of Les
Claypool's style, I will denote a down-stroke strum (played by
strumming with your picking hand fingernails) with a "V", and an
up-stroke strum (you can use your thumbnail, or the pads of your
fingertips) with a "^".  I will try to stick to the faq for the rest
of the notation, (or at least make it understandable).  Also, I will
indicate vibrato by writing "/\/\/\/\/\/\/" above the staff.  Triplets
will be indicated by "|-3-|" also above the staff, the triplets shoud
be played with three fingers, starting with your ring finger (that's
the easiest way I've found to play them).

opening riff: Played with first finger + thumb, classical guitar
style.  Fairly fast. Alternate between two of the first measure with
each of the three fills, do this twice, then alternate between the
first measure and the last fill until you want to start the main
riff.

This is a really easy riff to play, it's just that no one knows how to
play it.  What you do is tap along the neck with your right-hand middle
finger on the D string, and hammer the A and E strings.  I'm not quite
sure which notes to tap.  All I know is the second and last time he does
this section, tap on the 8th and 9th fret.  You'll get it.

Tabbed by Jared Duncan.

The opening line is played with the low note strummed with your thumb
and the high note strummed with the index fingger, in a back and forth
motion.  The rest of the song is with the low notes slapped and the
chords played flamenco style.  As for the little break in there, it is
tapped.
