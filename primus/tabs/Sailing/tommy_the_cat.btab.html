Primus - Tommy the Cat

Tabbed by Yocco. 


If your hand stops moving, you're not playing it right!
Play as percussive as possible.
The "x"'s with the "+" under them mean to click the strings
against the fretboard with your thumb. The "x"'s with the "v" and "^"'s
under them means to palm-mute the strings while strumming.  Did I loose you yet?

 Once you get the pattern down, it's easy!
B|---4-4-------------------------xx-3/4---------------------|
G|---4-4-------------------------xx-3/4---------------------|
D|--------------4/5-5-----5--------------------4/5-5-----5--|     This is just the first pattern.
A|----------------------------------------------------------|
E|--------x0xx--------x-3---xxxx---------00xx--------x-3----|
B|----------------------------------------------------------|
     v ^  ++++  -   - + + - ++++ v^ v    ++++  -   - + + -


 Now I'll throw in some variations. I recommend you bastardize it at will.
B|---4-4-------------------------xx-4-4--------9-9-8-7-6-6-5--xx4-4----------------------------|
G|---4-4-------------------------xx-4-4--------9-9-8-7-6-6-5--xx4-4----------------------------|
D|--------------4/5-5-----5------------------------------6-5-----------------------------------|    Do this every once
A|------------------------------------------------------------------xx-xx--------------2-3-5---|    in a while.
E|--------x0xx--------X-3---xxxx--------x0xx3/----------------------xx-xx-xx-----2-3-5---------|
B|------------------------------------------------------------------xx-xx---03-5---------------|
     v ^  ++++  -   - + + - ++++ v^ v ^ +++++  v ^ v ^ v ^ v  v^v ^ v^ v^ ++++ + + + + + + +

Primus - Tommy the Cat

Tabbed by Zoe Anne Nicolette Talbot Jenkins the 1st. 

Each dash (-) represents a 1/32 note space. 
So two = 1/16 note, four = 1/8 etc.
An X instead of a number denotes a muted note.
A ^ above a note indicates a harmonic.
Below the notes are the instructions for technique.
T = thumb slap, P(x)= snap with xth finger
GH = Ghost note, LH strikes fretboard for percussive mute
SU = strum up, SD = strum down
H = LH hammeron, SL = LH slide from previous note
R(x) = RH hammeron with xth finger
PO = pull off from previous note

The intro, and other parts, have some low B notes on 'em. Since I've
aquired a 5 string recently, I'll include them where appropriate. Thus
some bars have 5 tab lines. If you've got a 4-string, read only the
upper 4, and approximate the 5th(!)
