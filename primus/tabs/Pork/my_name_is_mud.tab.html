Comment by Andrew Reinartz. 

I discovered that no one really knows how to play the middle part of
My Name is Mud, the part where he's singing.  Here's how he does it.
I saw him do this on HBO Reverb (kicked ass), and it boggled my mind.
What he does is mute the strings either with the left hand or right
arm to a certain extent, and then he slaps about 1/2 way to 3/4 of the
way up the neck towards the nut.  If you see it you'd understand in an
instant.

Verse: This is the part that no one can get.  It's actually pretty
easy.  What you do is rest your forearm on the low B string.  Then,
rest your lest index finger on the 2nd fret.  Now, here's the trickey
part.  Slap the muted B TWICE with your right thumb, then hammer the B
with your remaining fingers on your left hand really hard.  Make sure
you hammer hard enough to get the same sound as your right hand, but
not so hard that you hammer the 3rd fret, and get a note out of it.
Try this a few times, very slowly, then gain speed as you get more
confident.  Pretty soon, it will become second nature, and you will do
it with no problem at all.  If you could only see how he does it,
you'd probably be kicking yourself in the head.  It's one of those
things where you need to see it to belive it.  Anyhow...

Through the whole thing, LIGHTLY press the frets, just enough to get
the note value.  Since Les plays this on a fretless, he probably has
no problem with this.  You want it to sound as "punchey" as possible.
More of a muted sound.  You've gotta have a FAST thumb, and a lot of
stamina to keep it up.
